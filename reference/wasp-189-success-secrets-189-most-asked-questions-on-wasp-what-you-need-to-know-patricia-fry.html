Now available, WASP. 'WAsP' (Wind Atlas Analysis and Application Program) is a PC programme for foreasting wind climate conditions, wind assets, and power performances as of wind generators and wind farms. The forecasts are founded on wind information calculated at meteorological stations in the similar area. The programme contains a compound land stream model, a roughness change model, a model for sheltering stumbling blocks, and a rouse model. The code bundle additional holds a Climate Analyst for generating the wind-climatological loads, a Map Editor for generating and correcting the Topographical loads, and a Turbine Editor for generating the wind turbine loads to WAsP. The basics of WAsP and the wind atlas collection of methods, practices, procedures and rules are depicted in the European Wind Atlas. WAsP is elaborated and dispersed by the Department of Wind Energy at the Technical University of Denmark, Denmark. Current variant is WAsP 11. There has never been a WASP Guide like this.
